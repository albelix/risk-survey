First, every participant will be randomly paired with another participant. In other words, you will have a counterpart, but you will not be told who it is. Your identity will also remain hidden from your counterpart.

The two participants in a pair will have two different roles: player 1 and player 2. You will be assigned randomly to a role, and it will be displayed at the beginning of the experiment. Your role will remain constant during the whole experiment.

The experiment consists of 3 games. Once again, your role will remain the same for all games. However, your counterpart will be chosen randomly for each game. Since the roles remain the same, your counterpart will be chosen among the participants who have the opposite role.

In each game, the two of you will together receive {{ Constants.endowment }} tokens and you have to decide how to divide this amount. The difference in games is in the rules.

Game 1

Player 1 makes an offer to player 2. This offer specifies how to divide the {{ Constants.endowment }} tokens between the two players. This goes as follows.

Player 1 will see the following {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} options:

    {% for give, keep in Constants.keep_give_amounts %}
  1. Player 2 gets {{ give }} tokens (you keep {{ keep }} tokens)
    2. {% endfor %}

Player 1 has to choose exactly one option by clicking on the button next to it (the program will not allow the player to choose more than one option or continue the game without choosing any). Player 1 can change his / her choice as many times as she / he wants: when a different option is chosen, the other one is automatically became unmarked. When the final choice is made, player 1 should click on "Submit" button at the left bottom corner of the screen.

While player 1 decides on the offer, player 2 should select which {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} offers stated above to accept or reject. This goes as follows.

Player 2 will see the following {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} options:

    {% for give, keep in Constants.keep_give_amounts %}
  1. You get {{ give }} tokens (player 1 keeps {{ keep }} tokens)
    2. {% endfor %}

For each option, there will be two buttons marked as "Accept" and "Reject." Exactly one button must be clicked for each option. Again, player 2 can change his / her choice as many times as she / he wants: when a different option is chosen, the other one is automatically became unmarked. When all {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} decisions are made, player 2 should click on "Submit" button at the left bottom corner of the screen.

The payoff in this game is defined as follows. If player 1's offer is accepted by player 2, the players receive their payoffs according to this offer. If player 1's offer is rejected by player 2, both players receive {{ Constants.payoff_if_rejected }} tokens.

When all participants in the experiment made their choices in game 1, game 2 starts. You will see your payoff from game 1 at the end of the experiment, that is, at the end of game 3.

Game 2

Player 1's screen in game 2 looks exactly the same as in game 1. However, player 2's screen is different.

Player 1 makes an offer to player 2. This offer specifies how to divide the {{ Constants.endowment }} tokens between the two players. This goes as follows.

Player 1 will see the following {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} options:

    {% for give, keep in Constants.keep_give_amounts %}
  1. Player 2 gets {{ give }} tokens (you keep {{ keep }} tokens)
    2. {% endfor %}

Player 1 has to choose exactly one option by clicking on the button next to it (the program will not allow the player to choose more than one option or continue the game without choosing any). Player 1 can change his / her choice as many times as she / he wants: when a different option is chosen, the other one is automatically became unmarked. When the final choice is made, player 1 should click on "Submit" button at the left bottom corner of the screen.

So far, everything is the same as in game 1. Here comes the difference between two games.

Player 2 does not make any choice in game 2. Any offer player 1 made is accepted automatically.

The payoff in this game is defined according to player 1's offer.

When all participant with player 1 role made their choices in game 2, game 3 starts. You will see your payoff from game 2 at the end of the experiment, that is, at the end of game 3.

Game 3

Player 2 has to choose which game to play, game 1 or game 2.

Player 2 will see two buttons marked as "Game 1" and "Game 2." After selecting the game, player 2 should click on "Submit" button at the left bottom corner of the screen.

If player 2 chose game 1, the next screen is displayed {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} options:

    {% for give, keep in Constants.keep_give_amounts %}
  1. You get {{ give }} tokens (player 1 keeps {{ keep }} tokens)
    2. {% endfor %}

For each option, there will be two buttons marked as "Accept" and "Reject." Exactly one button must be clicked for each option. When all {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} decisions are made, player 2 should click on "Submit" button at the left bottom corner of the screen.

If player 2 chose game 2, player 1's offer is accepted automatically and therefore there is nothing else to choose.

While player 2 decides on the game (and on acceptance decisions if game 1 is chosen), player 1 should decide how much to give to player 2 for each game.

Player 1 will see two lists, with {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} options each:

    {% for give, keep in Constants.keep_give_amounts %}
  1. Player 2 gets {{ give }} tokens (you keep {{ keep }} tokens)
    2. {% endfor %}

List 1 corresponds to game 1, list 2 corresponds to game 2. That means player 1 has to choose among {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} options twice: in the first list, player 1 chooses how much to give to player 2 if player 2 chooses game 1; in the second list, player 1 chooses how much to give to player 2 if player 2 chooses game 2.

The payoff in this game is defined as follows. If player 2 chose game 1 and player 1's offer from list 1 is accepted by player 2, the players receive their payoffs according to this offer. If player 2 chose game 1 and player 1's offer from list 1 is rejected by player 2, both players receive {{ Constants.payoff_if_rejected }} tokens. If player 2 chose game 2, the players receive their payoffs according to player 1's offer from list 2.

When all participants in the experiment made their choices in game 3, you will see your payoff from all three games.

Timeline of the experiment

  1. After reading the instructions, each of you receive a small quiz that checks your understanding of these instructions. You will be asked to answer all questions in the quiz. Then your answers will be checked, and all mistakes will be explained. Your payment does not depend how well you perform on this quiz. The only purpose of this quiz is to eliminate any confusion you might have about the experimental design.
  2. Then the experiment starts.
    1. Every participant will be randomly paired with another participant. You will not see who is paired with whom. Therefore, you do not know who your counterpart is.
    2. You are told whether you are player 1 or player 2. This assignment will never change.
    3. Game 1 starts. Depending on your role:
      • Player 1: Choose how much to offer to player 2.
      • Player 2: For each of {{ Constants.offer_choices_count }} options, decide whether to accept the offer or reject it.
      You will observe your payoff from game 1 at the end of the experiment.
    4. Game 2 starts. Your new counterpart is chosen randomly among the participants who have the opposite role.
    5. Depending on your role:
      • Player 1: Choose how much to offer to player 2.
      • Player 2: Do nothing.
      You will observe your payoff from game 2 at the end of the experiment.
    6. Game 3 starts. Your new counterpart is chosen randomly among the participants who have the opposite role.
    7. Depending on your role:
      • Player 1: For each of two games, choose how much to offer to player 2.
      • Player 2: Choose between two games, game 1 and game 2. If you choose game 1, you should also indicate your response to the first offer: For each of 11 options, decide whether to accept the offer or reject it.
    8. You observe your payoff from game 1, game 2, and game 3 separately.
    9. For each participant, the program chooses randomly (with equal probability) what game should be taken for the actual payment. In other words, exactly one of three games will be chosen to determine how much you get from this experiment.
  3. After completing the experiment, you will be asked to answer a few more questions. You will be paid for some of them as well.
