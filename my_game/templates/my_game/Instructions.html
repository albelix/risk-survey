Instructions

First, every player will be randomly paired with another player. In other words, you will have a counterpart, but you will not be told who it is. Your identity will also remain hidden from your counterpart.

The two participants in a pair will have two different roles: the proposer and the responder. You will be assigned randomly to a role, and it will be displayed on the next page.

There will be {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds of the game. Your role will remain the same for all rounds. However, your counterpart will be chosen randomly each round among the players who have the opposite role.

In each round, the two of you will together receive {{ Constants.endowment }}. The experiment is about how to divide this amount.

The game consists of two stages.

Stage 1

First the responder makes a choice between two options: RESPOND or ACCEPT. This choice is conditional on the computer's choice between INFORMED or UNINFORMED. That means the responder has to choose between RESPOND or ACCEPT twice: first, for the scenario when the computer chooses INFORMED and second, for the other scenario when the computer chooses UNINFORMED.

Then the computer uniformly randomly chooses among six options:

  • (INFORMED,RESPONDER'S CHOICE),
  • (INFORMED,RESPOND),
  • (INFORMED,ACCEPT),
  • (UNINFORMED,RESPONDER'S CHOICE),
  • (UNINFORMED,RESPOND),
  • (UNINFORMED,ACCEPT).

Based on the responder's and the computer's choices, it is decided what type of the game will be played in stage 2, RESPOND or ACCEPT.

  • If the computer chose (INFORMED,RESPONDER'S CHOICE), then the type G is defined by the responder's choice conditional on INFORMED.
  • If the computer chose (UNINFORMED,RESPONDER'S CHOICE), then the type G is defined by the responder's choice conditional on UNINFORMED.
  • If the computer chose RESPOND, then G=RESPOND.
  • If the computer chose ACCEPT, then G=ACCEPT.

Both players are informed about the type of the game G. In addition, if the computer chose INFORMED, they both know whether the choice of the game was made by the computer (when the computer chose RESPOND or ACCEPT) or by the responder (when the computer chose RESPONDER'S CHOICE).
